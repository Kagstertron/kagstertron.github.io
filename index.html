Esther the Collector | Magic the Gathering

Magic: the Gathering is the trading card game like no other. Wizards of the Coast released the first set of cards in 1993. This set is now known as 'Alpha', and the cards are valued today anywhere from £20 - £3million.

The game drew inspiration heavily from other RPGs, however the gameplay and complexity is vastly different. The premis for the game is wizards dueling on a battlefield - the cards representing a wizard's spells. Alongside the cards, there is a rich wealth of lore in the form of novels, comics and an official webseries.

Since its inception, billions of cards have been printed for the now 50million global fans of the card game (as of 2023). And those fans are what makes the game so special: the unwashed, over-caffeinated, and under-socialised player-base that gather in their local game shops, ripe with a barely supressed air of misogyny.


Owned by Hasbro, the toy-making giant has become increasingly reliant on the success of Magic the Gathering - which saw record-breaking revenue in 2025. Wizards, that also own D&D and the digital gaming division of Wizards of the Coast, saw a total revenue of $2.2bn.

This success is partly due to the game's recent sets known as Universes Beyond: combining the mechanics of Magic with other fantasy worlds like Middle-earth, Cybertron and Marvel.
