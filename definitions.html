Esther the Collector | Definitions

  • The Library:

    • Imagine you are a magician with a huge catalogue of spells at your disposal. To help you remember, all your spells are written on cards in a deck. This is your library.

  • Graveyard:

    • Every used spell – whether removed, destroyed, fizzled or finished – goes to the graveyard.

  • Mana:

    • To be able to cast spells from your library, you need enough mana to pay the spells cost – nothing comes free! You gather mana by playing lands.

  • The Battlefield:

    • The battlefield is your grand staging area where all spells are cast.

  • Tap:

    • To attack or activate a spell on your battlefield, rotate it 45 degrees.

  • Permanent:

    • A permanent is any spell from your library that remains on the battlefield once cast (mostly excluding instances & sorceries).

  • Land:

    • Mana comes from lands in 5 basic forms, drawing power from the world around you. Plains provide white mana; islands blue; swamps black; mountains red and forests green. You can shape your environment by drawing the right land cards from your library; you can only play 1 land per turn.

  • Creature:

    • Creatures will have a power and toughness allocated to them enabling them to fight for and defend you. If a creature’s toughness equals 0 that means lights out, I’m afraid. But remember they fought valiantly for you. And remember, being cast from a spellbook makes you queasy, so unless a creature has haste, it has summoning sickness until your next turn.

  • Enchantment:

    • An enchantment stays on the battlefield and will grant you some kind of strategic advantage – these can come in different formats. These can only be destroyed by a spell that specifically targets permanents or enchantments.

  • Sorcery:

    • Sorceries change the battle in some way, giving you an advantage or targeting your opponents’ spells. But remember, sorceries must be played in your main phase.

  • Instant:

    • Instants are like sorceries but better! They can be played at almost any time – including on your opponents’ turns!

  • Planeswalker:

    • A planeswalker spell is almost like bringing another player into the mix (on your side, don’t worry!). Their loyalty points act like a life total and your opponent must declare they’re attacking your planeswalker specifically to do them any damage. You can also increase or decrease your planeswalker’s loyalty points in exchange for spells of their own.
